Information technology I

This course is an introductory computer science course that empowers students to create authentic artifacts and engage with computer science as a medium for creativity, communication, problem solving, and fun. Students learn HTML, CSS and Python.

Information technology II

This course introduces students to the fundamentals of computer hardware and software. Topics covered are mobile devices, Linux, macOS, virtualization, and cloud computing as well as expanded information about Microsoft Windows operating systems, security, networking, troubleshooting, and the responsibilities of an IT professional.

Information technology III

Students will gain hands-on experience in verifying and addressing network and internet connectivity issues and identifying and addressing security threats to the network. The course will cover the basics of cybersecurity and its potential implications for individuals, including the most prevalent threats, attacks, and vulnerabilities that users encounter.
